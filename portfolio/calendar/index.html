Where To Travel In 2019?

A month by month guide of where to visit this year - and when!

Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday Sunday

January

Ring in the New Year around the world, or celebrate some amazing festivals, sites and visit places that shine in January. Whether you're living it up on the beach, checking out amazing firework displays or rocking a party, here's the best of January!

Head to Bora Bora (pictured below) for sunny skies, a beautiful lagoon and out of this world luxury.

Venture to Scandinavia or Northern Canada to check out the northern lights and beautiful snowy scenery.

Festival Time: Check out Scotland's fire festival, Up Hella Aa, to commemorate the end of the Yule season.

February

Though a dark, dreary month in Canada, February has tons to offer for travellers. While snow coats the northern hemisphere, great weather welcomes you to the Caribbean and much of Latin America. Take advantage of cold weather sports or ditch it for clear skies.

Worldclass sunsets, waves and adventure awaits in Costa Rica. Pictured below: Nosara, Costa Rica at sunset.

Famous among skiier's, Japan's powder snow days are legendary. The food, culture and scenery are just a bonus.

Come for the skiing, stay for the festivities. Sapporo Snow Festival offers food, fun and lots of snowy activities!

March

From Carnival and Mardi Gras, St. Patricks Day, Holi, Las Fallas and Balinese New Year, March 2019 is a month chaulk full of cultural celebrations. Pack a bag and get ready for a celebration, on almost every continent around the world.

Hitting the perfect shoulder season (and just in time for Carnival) head to Northern Brazil, pictured below.

Celebrate St. Patricks Day with the Irish and as a bonus, beautiful uncrowed sightseeing, with just a little rain.

From the parade at the Sambadromo to street parties, enjoy the ultimate Carnival celebration in Rio de Janeiro.

April

The ultimate shoulder season, April provides perfectly imperfect trips in so many hot spots around the world. With shoulder season comes lower prices, few tourists and more opportunities to connect with locals.

Though a little rainy, Ecuador and The Galapagos, pictured below, provide low prices and great temperatures in April.

As the winter melts, head to Iceland to see waterfalls in full force and lower prices after a busy winter.

All over the Netherlands, King's Day is celebrated with street parties, concerts and plenty of orange attire.

May

Another fantastic shoulder season to travel in much of the world, May provides tons of opportunity get off the beaten path, and beat the summer crowds. Enjoy uncrowded monuments in Europe, Buddhist celebrations all over Asia and perfect weather in much of the world.

Head to the Caribbean for off season prices with continually beautiful weather. Aruba is still in dry season.

Enjoy the crisp, cold mountain air of Ladakh, India beofre the summer hiking season begins.

Travel to Java to learn about Waisak, Buddha Day, or trek to nearby Mount Bromo, pictured below

June

The beginnings of winter in the southern hemisphere and summer in the north, June brings great weather for trekking, outdoor activities and scenery. From Oceania's winter to summer in Europe, June is a great time to travel.

Scandinavia's long summer days, summer solstice celebrations and great festivals await in Norther Europe.

Heading into New Zealand's winter, enjoy sunny fresh days and cool nights and the beginning of the ski season.

Machu Picchu, pictured below, shines all year sound but June provides cooler weather, less tourists and sunny skies.

July

Summer is in full swing in the Northern Hemisphere, but that's no reason to not get off the beaten path. Hot spots in South East Asia are also in high season, with the dry winter providing great weather. From classic European summer trips to ancient festivals, enjoy everything July has to offer.

Head to Bali, pictured below, for great weather and tons to do from festivals to adventure activities.

Take advantage of the short season for skiing in Australia with a trip to New South Wales.

Dating back to the 13th Century, Naddam Festival in Mongolia celebrates wrestling, horse racing and archery.

August

As summer weather continues in the Canada and Europe, those looking for snow can head south to Argentina or Chile. The tropics of the South Pacific are also in high season, in their dry winter days. Check out tons of summer festivals and outdoor trip opportunities.

Though their winter, the Cook Islands, pictured below, is a perfect destination for August at a balmy 28 degrees.

Argentina's mountains were overlooked for too long by skiiers. Attractions are also less crowded due to the cold.

Burning Man takes over Nevadas' Black Rock Desert every August for a temporary radical metropolis.

September

As autumn approaches, take advantage of cooling temperatures in the north and lesser crowds in Japan, Europe and the United States as students head back to school. The weather will be a little less predictable, but clear skies and beautiful days make up for the rain.

Lesser crowds make Japan, pictured below, a great choice for enjoying amazing food and a fascinating culture.

A time of transformation, September in Alaska brings changing colours, light snowfalls and long days.

Celebrated across Germany and the world, Oktoberfest is not to be missed for great food and the best party around.

October

Getting away in October is a wonderful idea as we come into winter. Prices across Central America and the Caribbean are reasonable after hurricane season but towns are not yet crowded for the busy winter. Southern countries come into winter, providing lots of great hiking opportunities.

After a hot summer, October in the Yucatan Peninsula, picture below, offers low prices before high season.

Beautiful clear and chilly days spread across Patagonia and the lack of crowds is reason enough to visit.

The Hindu festival of lights, Diwali is celebrated for 5 days across India with family, food and festivities.

November

Summer reigns in the Southern Hemisphere with great weather and lots to do. In the north, find changing colours and snow beginning to fall bringing lots of outdoor activities and fun.

Cape Town, South Africa, pictured below, comes into summer with great weather, festivals and tons to do.

Autumn in Whistler bridges beautiful hiking trails with the start of the ski season. As a bonus, prices are lower.

More than just Hallowe'en, Day Of The Dead in Mexico is a celebration of life and death dating back to the Aztecs.

December

Celebrate Christmas around the world, from sunny skies in Australia to classic snowy festivities in Europe. This time of year also brings prime beach weather from Thailand to the Caribbean and southern Africa, there's so many places to explore in December.

A country of beautiful, cheap beach destinations, Malaysia offers great value and weather this month.

Head as far south as possible to Antarctica and take in the pristine landscapes and sunny, yet cold, days.

Celebrate throughout Europe with Christmas Markets and great ski conditions in the alps, pictured below.
